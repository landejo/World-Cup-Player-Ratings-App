How the rating works

Replicable Weighted-Action Model · v4.4

The idea

Every player starts from a position-specific par and earns credit for weighted actions pulled straight from the FIFA Post-Match Summary — progressive passing, line breaks, ball progression, take-ons, duels, blocks, interceptions, pressures and clearances. Goalkeepers run on a separate save-quality + distribution formula.

Scores are floored at 5.0 for any appearance, a goal is worth up to +1.8 (scorers floored at 7.0), and the result nudges everyone by +0.30 / 0 / −0.30 for a win / draw / loss.

Team rating

The /100 figure is the minutes-weighted mean of all player ratings (including the result adjustment), ×10 — so dominant, winning teams score higher.

Calibrated on three reference-rated matches and cross-validated to a ~0.32 mean absolute error — 86% of players land within 0.5 of the human grade (the model's own target is 0.51). Positions and minutes are read straight from the official team sheet — tap any player card to see the full stat breakdown behind their score.
